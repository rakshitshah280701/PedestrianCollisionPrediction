Introduction

Welcome to this online tutorial that reviews the state-of-the-art paper titled “Pedestrian Collision Prediction Using a Monocular Camera”, published in the iWOAR 2023 conference. This paper introduces PedView, a novel system that leverages a monocular camera and fuzzy logic to accurately predict pedestrian collisions, designed for integration into Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS). In this tutorial, we’ll walk you through the technical details, algorithms, experiments, and more.

Abstract

The paper introduces a simple yet efficient method, PedView, for pedestrian collision warning in Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS). Unlike existing approaches relying on LiDAR and stereo cameras, PedView uses a forward-looking monocular camera for 3D pedestrian detection. It incorporates pedestrian location and intent using fuzzy rules, significantly improving collision prediction accuracy. Extensive experiments on two datasets demonstrate PedView's superior performance compared to traditional methods, making it particularly suitable for resource-limited environments. This tutorial will delve into the system’s design, algorithms, and its experimental performance.
